I came across a paper this week I want to share. Published this year in Scientific Reports. It landed for me.

The finding: when someone is in a situation that doesn't fit who they are, it doesn't just wear them down. It starts producing behavior that wouldn't otherwise be there. Aggressive behavior. Self-sabotage. Disengagement. The kind of thing that looks like character.

It isn't character. The situation is doing it.

You know this pattern. The client who's become difficult, whose behavior doesn't match what you know about them. The instinct — theirs and sometimes ours — is to ask what's wrong with the person. What's the block. What changed in them.

The research asks a different question: what is the situation doing to them?

I've been paying more attention to what situations produce in people lately, not just what people bring to them. This paper is another piece of that.

Full paper: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-09700-w
