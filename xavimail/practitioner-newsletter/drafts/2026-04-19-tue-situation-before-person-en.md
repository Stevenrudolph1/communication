Subject: What to look at before you look at the person

Hi {first_name},

Most of what clients bring you is described as a person problem. They're struggling. They're stuck. They don't know what to do.

The first practitioner instinct is to look at the person — their Natures, their Intelligences, their history. That instinct is trained into us. It's also usually the wrong starting move.

The situation came before the symptoms. Whatever the client is experiencing — the exhaustion, the confusion, the inability to move — is almost always a response to what their situation has been asking of them. Look at that first.

What does this role actually demand each day? Who does the client have to be in this context? What does the situation recognize, reward, ignore, penalize?

Once that's clear, the client's response to it usually makes sense without much explanation. The struggle isn't mysterious. It's what happens when certain things are asked of certain people for long enough.

That sequence changes what you're listening for from the first session. Not "what's wrong with this person" — but "what has this situation been doing."
