## Publish Gate (MANDATORY per article)

Every article must pass all four checks before publishing:

1. **Plain-language pass** — no framework jargon, no esoteric terms, no insider language. A smart HR leader who's never heard of Renergence must understand every sentence.
2. **≥2 concrete examples** — real scenarios, specific details, recognizable situations. No abstractions standing alone.
3. **Single clear CTA** — one link, one action. No menu of options.
4. **Companion post + comment response plan + DM trigger** — post drafted, first-comment link ready, plan for responding to commenters, DM trigger criteria defined (feeds T-027 CRM system).

## Article Architecture (Template)

Every LinkedIn Article follows this structure:

1. **Hook** (2-3 sentences) — specific scenario or observation, no abstraction
2. **The Problem** — what everyone assumes vs. what's actually happening
3. **The Framework** — introduce the diagnostic lens (S/A/P, MN, or hero trap)
4. **One Example** — concrete, specific, recognizable
5. **The Distinction** — the named mechanism that makes this different from generic advice
6. **CTA** — one clear link to xavigate.com resource

**Voice:** Same as Substack — declarative, structural, no self-help, no prescriptions. The Substack Publishing Canon applies.

**Format rules:**
- Headers (H2) for sections
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- No bullet-point listicles
- No emoji
- One embedded link to xavigate.com, one to the relevant book
