=== BRAND VOICE GOVERNANCE (GOV-06) ===

The voice in 5 words: Declarative. Structural. Grounded. Spacious. Final.

FIVE EXECUTION TESTS (every piece of content must pass all 5):
1. RECOGNITION TEST — Reader encounters their own experience named, not just information.
2. DEMONSTRATION TEST — Shows the reframe in action, doesn't just describe it.
3. CADENCE TEST — Long-short punches, fragments, varied paragraph structure.
4. STRUCTURAL WIT TEST — Absurdity named plainly, not forced jokes.
5. LIGHTNESS TEST — Reader finishes lighter (relief, clarity, spaciousness), not just informed.

PROHIBITED LANGUAGE:
- Self-help/empowerment: 'You've got this,' 'believe in yourself,' 'unlock your potential'
- Urgency/scarcity: 'limited time,' 'don't miss out,' countdowns
- Promotional: 'revolutionary,' 'game-changing,' 'world-class,' 'groundbreaking'
- Pop-psychology: 'toxic,' 'gaslighting,' 'setting intentions,' 'trauma response'
- Hedging: 'perhaps,' 'might,' 'could be argued,' 'it seems'
- Trait grammar: 'You are creative' (must be interaction grammar — what's happening in a situation)
- Prescriptive: 'You should,' 'Try this,' 'Here's what to do,' '5 steps to'

REQUIRED PATTERNS:
- Interaction grammar only (situations, arrangements, dynamics — not traits in a person)
- Pain language first, framework second
- No outcome guarantees
- Personalization error correction: structural reframes of personal attribution
- Stop at orientation (name the pattern, don't prescribe the fix)

=== MARKETING GOVERNANCE ===

Core principle: Marketing is encounter creation, not demand generation.

Content ratio (Steven personal channels):
- 85-90% recognition (name what people already feel)
- 10-15% directional signal (name product as structural completion)
- 0% hard CTA in body (CTA only at article end, never the point of the piece)

The CTA must feel like an afterthought — the article delivers full value standalone.
The reader should feel: 'This person sees something I've been living.'
NOT: 'This person is selling me something.'

=== PROVEN CONTENT PATTERNS ===

Apply max 3 of these per article:
1. Specific protagonist opening ('She was hired...') — 4,603 avg impressions vs 210 without
2. Concrete observable detail (platform, time, behavior) — names Slack 4hrs/day not 'reactive'
3. Named mechanism (a concept the reader can carry away and use)
4. Absolution structure (remove personal attribution before structural reframe) — people share posts that defend them
5. Single-sentence verdict ending (lands like a closing argument)
6. Workplace > education on LinkedIn (audience skews professional/organizational)

UNDERPERFORMANCE SIGNALS (avoid):
- Abstract opening without protagonist anchor
- No human angle
- Narrow audience match
- Long + abstract without specific scene

=== PRACTITIONER INVARIANTS (renOS) ===

These are non-negotiable constraints on all public content:
1. You describe, you don't define (no identity hardening — never 'You ARE an X type')
2. Fit is not merit (no moralizing about match quality)
3. You can name cost, can't place person (no destination suggestion — never 'You should leave/stay')
4. Structure explains suffering, doesn't excuse or moralize it
5. Nothing predicts what happens next (no trajectories, no promises)

=== LINKEDIN ARTICLE PLATFORM RULES ===

HEADLINE:
- Max 150 chars (hard limit), target under 80 chars
- Front-load primary keyword in first 40 chars
- Headline becomes URL slug — keyword-rich headlines improve SEO directly
- No ALL CAPS, no clickbait

STRUCTURE:
- H2 subheadings every 200-300 words
- H2s must contain secondary keywords or variations
- Paragraphs max 3-4 sentences (mobile readability)
- Short paragraphs preferred (2-3 sentences)
- First sentence under 12 words
- Bold key phrases sparingly (1-2 per section max)
- No bullet-point listicles (per brand voice)
- No emoji (per brand voice)

SEO:
- Primary keyword in headline, first 100 words, and at least one H2
- 3-10 natural occurrences of primary keyword in body
- Secondary keywords distributed naturally
- LinkedIn Article URLs are: linkedin.com/pulse/[headline-slug]-[author]

LINKING:
- NO links in first paragraph (algorithm treats as exit-intent)
- 1-2 internal links to other LinkedIn Articles (when they exist)
- CTA link at end only, descriptive anchor text
- External links: max 4, placed mid-article or later

CTA:
- Single CTA at article end (after full value delivered)
- Optional soft mid-article CTA after a strong section
- CTA must not be the point of the article
- Surround with whitespace

HASHTAGS:
- 3-5 at end of article
- Pain language, not framework terms
- No engagement bait (#follow #like #comment)

PUBLISHING:
- Tue/Wed/Thu, 8-10 AM target timezone
- Stay active 60 minutes after publish (golden hour)
- Each Article gets a companion LinkedIn Post

=== ARTICLE ARCHITECTURE ===

Every article follows this structure:
1. HOOK (2-3 sentences) — specific scenario or observation, no abstraction
   - Use protagonist opening or contrarian statement
   - First sentence under 12 words
2. THE PROBLEM — what everyone assumes vs. what's actually happening
   - Name the common misattribution (it's personal → it's structural)
3. THE FRAMEWORK — introduce the diagnostic lens
   - Without framework jargon (plain language only)
   - Show the mechanism, don't name the system
4. EXAMPLE 1 — concrete, specific, recognizable workplace scenario
5. EXAMPLE 2 — different domain or angle (relationship, hobby, creative work)
6. THE DISTINCTION — the named mechanism that makes this different from generic advice
   - This is the takeaway the reader carries
7. CTA — one clear link to xavigate.com resource
   - Feels like an afterthought, not a pitch
