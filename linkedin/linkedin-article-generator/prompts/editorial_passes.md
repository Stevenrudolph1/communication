# Editorial Pass Definitions

Each article goes through 5 sequential editing passes. Each pass has a specific focus, scoring rubric, and failure criteria.

---

## Pass 1: VOICE

**Focus:** GOV-06 brand voice compliance

**Check each sentence against:**
- Is it declarative (not tentative, not hedging)?
- Is it structural (about the situation, not the person's traits)?
- Is it grounded (specific, observable, not abstract)?
- Does it avoid ALL prohibited language patterns?
- Does it use interaction grammar (not trait grammar)?

**Scoring (0-100):**
- 95-100: Pure Steven voice. Every sentence earns its place.
- 85-94: Strong voice with 1-2 minor deviations. Fixable.
- 70-84: Voice drift. Multiple sentences feel generic, advisory, or promotional.
- Below 70: Rewrite required. Voice is not present.

**Failure triggers (instant rewrite of affected section):**
- Any self-help language detected
- Any prescriptive advice ("you should," "try this")
- Any trait grammar ("you are a...")
- Any promotional language ("revolutionary," etc.)

---

## Pass 2: CLARITY

**Focus:** Plain language accessibility

**The test:** Could a smart HR leader who has never heard of Renergence, Multiple Natures, or Steven Rudolph understand every sentence on first read?

**Check for:**
- Framework jargon used without explanation (S/A/P, renergence, mode collapse, etc.)
- Insider references that assume prior knowledge
- Sentences that require re-reading to parse
- Abstract passages with no concrete anchor
- Run-on sentences or overly complex syntax

**Scoring (0-100):**
- 95-100: Crystal clear. A new reader gets full value.
- 85-94: Minor jargon or one unclear passage.
- 70-84: Multiple sections assume insider knowledge.
- Below 70: Inaccessible to target audience.

**Failure triggers:**
- Any Renergence-specific term used without plain-language explanation
- Any sentence requiring specialized knowledge to parse
- Paragraphs of 5+ sentences

---

## Pass 3: PLATFORM

**Focus:** LinkedIn-specific formatting and optimization

**Check:**
- Headline: under 80 chars (target), under 150 (hard limit)
- First sentence: under 12 words
- H2 frequency: every 200-300 words
- H2 content: contains keyword variations
- Paragraph length: max 4 sentences, prefer 2-3
- Bold usage: 1-2 per section, not whole sentences
- Link placement: none in first paragraph, CTA at end
- Hashtags: 3-5, pain language, at article end
- No emoji, no bullet lists, no numbered lists in body
- No engagement bait
- Word count within target range

**Scoring (0-100):**
- 95-100: Perfectly formatted for LinkedIn Articles.
- 85-94: Minor formatting issues (one long paragraph, etc.)
- 70-84: Multiple formatting violations.
- Below 70: Needs structural reformatting.

---

## Pass 4: SEO

**Focus:** Search engine optimization for Google indexation

**Check:**
- Primary keyword in: headline, first 100 words, at least one H2
- Primary keyword frequency: 3-10 natural occurrences
- Secondary keywords: present in body and/or H2s
- Headline is URL-friendly (becomes slug)
- No keyword stuffing (reads naturally)
- H2s are descriptive and keyword-aware

**Scoring (0-100):**
- 95-100: SEO-optimized without compromising voice.
- 85-94: One keyword placement missing.
- 70-84: Multiple SEO gaps.
- Below 70: Not optimized. Will not rank.

**Keyword stuffing check:** If primary keyword appears >10 times in a 1,500-word article, reduce. SEO must never override voice.

---

## Pass 5: CADENCE

**Focus:** Steven's writing rhythm and the 5 execution tests

**Check against GOV-06 execution tests:**
1. RECOGNITION — Does the reader encounter their own experience named?
2. DEMONSTRATION — Does the article show the reframe in action (not just describe it)?
3. CADENCE — Long-short sentence variation? Fragments? Paragraph variety?
4. STRUCTURAL WIT — Is absurdity named plainly (not as a joke)?
5. LIGHTNESS — Does the reader finish lighter? With relief or clarity?

**Also check:**
- Sentence length variation (mix of 5-word and 20-word sentences)
- At least one fragment or short standalone sentence per section
- Opening and closing sentences are the strongest in each section
- The final paragraph lands — single-sentence verdict energy
- No two consecutive paragraphs of the same length

**Scoring (0-100):**
- 95-100: Could be a page from the book. Rhythm is alive.
- 85-94: Good rhythm with 1-2 flat sections.
- 70-84: Rhythm is uneven. Some sections feel like a different writer.
- Below 70: Rewrite. The cadence is generic.

---

## Overall Pass/Fail

**Threshold: 85 across all 5 passes.**

If any pass scores below 85, that section is revised and re-scored. Maximum 3 revision rounds per pass. If still below 85 after 3 rounds, flag for human review.

**Final output includes:** All 5 pass scores, revision notes, and any flagged sections.
